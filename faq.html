Frequently Asked Questions

Will I ever not have access to my data?

Be Better!

No. In the event of cancellation of service, the Trinity files would remain in their compressed state but lose access to Trinity features, such as indexability. The customer can always open the files to return them to their pre-Trinity form.

_____

What does "passthrough system" mean?

Show the world!

Customer data is run through the Trinity process, converting it to a managed file. Via machine learning, the data is then returned to the most efficient location in the customer's storage.

_____

Why is Trinity hardware dependent and why does Validiti require control of the hardware?

Be Better!

Trinity is not dependent on hardware; it is software that operates like a driver. While there are substantial protections built in to protect the source code, we will not allow anything to operate between Trinity and the electrons in the computer. That logical point of weakness is unnecessary.

_____

Why isn't Trinity Open Source?

Show the world!

Trinity is the product of years of sacrifice and development. We had to solve many "impossible" problems in order to reach this point and it would be foolish to just turn that over. Trinity is protected as a trade secret similar to the Google Search Algo, WD40, and the recipe for Coca-Cola. In fact, Trinity is kinda like Coca-Cola, but still with cocaine.

_____

Trinity offers a lot of value propositions. Is it right for my business?

Show the world!

Yes. Our emphasis on the compression is based upon the needs of our initial customers. The security, FAP, etc offer tremendous value and can be demonstrated.

_____

What are the possibilities with Trinity?

Show the world!

Trinity is an engine for customers to use. We anticipate that people will find any number of firm and industry-specific opportunities that we can't imagine. The impact of lighter, faster, and more secure data and data processing isn't limited to the digital world.
